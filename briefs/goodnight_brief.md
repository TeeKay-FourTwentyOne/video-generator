# GOODNIGHT — Shooting Brief v1

**Date:** 2026-05-31
**Slug:** `goodnight` — workspace `data/workspace/goodnight/`
**Format:** 9:16 vertical, 4K final (Real-ESRGAN ×2 from 1080)
**Runtime target:** ~42s
**Lens:** Quiet dread / uncanny — horror of the mundane. No gore. No VFX. The reveal is a clean wide shot, fully renderable as ordinary footage.
**Pipeline:** Veo 3.1 Fast (book-ended off nano-banana poster frames) for every talking beat; **1 Quality reserved for the money shot (B7, the wide reveal)**. Veo **native audio ON** for the spoken goodnights (her voice is the whole point). Designed score/ambience layered in post.

---

## Logline

A woman records a tender "going to bed, goodnight, love you" video message to camera in a softly lit bedroom — and then records it again, and again, the exact same words to different names, until a wide shot reveals a row of propped phones all filming the same goodnight. Nobody is home. She is manufacturing "a normal night in" for many people at once.

## The turn (the whole piece)

The content is the most reassuring thing on earth: *goodnight, I'm safe, I'm home, sleep well.* The dread is **repetition + recontextualization**. We believe it's one sincere message to one person. The cut reveals it's a performance run on a loop — same script, swapped name, swapped cardigan — and the final wide shows the assembly line of it. The horror is the **labor of normalcy**: someone is being paid, or compelled, to be everyone's safe, ordinary evening. We never explain who or why. The unease is that the most comforting words in the language are being produced like a shift.

This is renderable because the reveal is a **physical fact in a wide shot** (multiple propped phones, multiple recording lights), not a transformation. No morph, no VFX, no body-horror. Reveal-on-the-frame, the way THE LODGER did reveal-on-the-cut.

## Why this fits the lens & the generator

- **Veo Fast's exact wheelhouse:** one human performer, deadpan-warm, talking to a propped phone in a real lamplit room. Native synced dialogue is excellent here, and the flat sincerity is *easier* for Fast than big emotion.
- **No failure modes invoked:** no neon/holo/glowing/screens-with-content (the phone faces away or is dark), no transformation/morph, no body-horror, no RAI-tripping prose (no violence/child-bodypart/nudity words). The script is all comfort words.
- **The identity anchor is the point.** Same woman every beat — char-lock hard. The only changes are wardrobe (cardigan color) and the name she says, both trivially controllable.

## Tone & palette

Warm, intimate, ASMR-cozy on the surface — the visual language of a sincere goodnight video / "get ready with me, going to bed" content. Bedside-lamp amber, soft pillow whites, a little bloom. Shallow depth. The camera is the phone: locked, slightly low, framed like she propped it on the nightstand. **The comfort of the look is the trap.** The grade never goes cold or horror-blue — the dread comes entirely from repetition and the reveal, not from lighting tricks. (If anything, the reveal wide is *brighter and flatter* — fluorescent-ish, the cozy lamp was a set dressing.)

## The woman — char-lock hard (`REF-WOMAN`)

One performer, present-day, mid-30s, warm and plain-pretty, the face of someone you'd trust to housesit. Soft features, light makeup, hair down or loosely up. **Lock three views (front / 3⁄4 / profile).** Her delivery is *almost* perfect — warm, practiced, a half-beat too even. She is very good at this. The flaw the viewer half-notices: her warmth never reaches a flicker of fatigue until the very end.

Wardrobe across beats: same woman, **rotating soft cardigans** — oatmeal → dusty rose → sage → grey. This is the only "tell" before the reveal: a careful eye clocks the wardrobe changing between identical messages.

## The room — char-lock (`REF-ROOM`)

A warm, generic, *aggressively cozy* bedroom: upholstered headboard, soft throw, a single warm lamp, a few neutral props (a plant, a framed photo turned just enough that we can't read the face, a water glass). **It reads as "a nice normal bedroom" and is deliberately anonymous** — no personal specificity, because it's a set. The framed photo never resolves. In the reveal wide, we see this same "bedroom" is a corner of a flat, bright, undecorated room — the cozy was a three-foot-deep set.

## Audio

Native Veo audio ON for B1, B2, B4, B6, B7 (her spoken goodnights — the voice *is* the piece; do not replace with TTS). Room tone, lamp hum, the soft tap of a phone screen, the scratch of pen on the pad — let Veo generate these in the talking beats; design/layer the inserts (B3, B5) in post.

- **Score:** almost nothing. A single, soft, sustained warm tone — a lullaby pad that is *too* soothing, like hold music for comfort. It should be pleasant in B1 and faintly nauseating by B6 through pure repetition (same two notes, looping). ElevenLabs. The score never "stings" the reveal — the dread is dry. *Optional:* the pad keeps looping unbroken under the cut-to-black, so her restarting voice and the lullaby just… continue. The loop is the horror.
- **The cut-to-black** has no music sting — just her voice starting again and then silence. Dry.
- **NO horror stingers, no drones-of-doom, no whoosh.** The lens is quiet dread; the moment we add a horror cue we've told the audience how to feel and killed it. Keep it cozy-flat throughout.

## Production notes

- **Lock the woman and the room before any shot.** The entire effect depends on her being unmistakably the *same person* delivering the *same script* — Veo Fast identity drift would read as "different videos," not "same woman on a loop," and collapse the gag. Three-view char-lock, book-end every talking beat with matched A/B posters.
- **Wardrobe = the only between-beat variable** besides the name. Pre-plan the cardigan colors (oatmeal/rose/sage/grey) into the nano posters.
- **The phone never shows screen content** (Veo screens-with-content failure mode). It's propped facing her, back to us, or dark. The recording "dot" in B7 is a tiny practical red point in the nano poster, not a rendered UI.
- **B7 reveal is the one Quality shot.** It's the money shot and the only one with set-geography complexity (row of phones, the set-within-a-room). Reserve Quality here; everything else is Fast.
- **B5 names list:** hand-letter via nano as plain first names, a few struck through. Keep them ordinary (Daniel, Priya, Mr. Halloran already used; add e.g. Susan, Tomás, Aunt Reni). Ordinary names are scarier than ominous ones.

## Risk notes

- **Riskiest beat: B7 reveal.** The "set within a bare room + row of propped phones" is the only spatially-complex shot. Mitigation: stage it dead simple — a single locked wide, the cozy set clearly at frame edge, 3–4 phones on visible mini-tripods in a line on a folding table, her seated. If Veo struggles, fall back to **building B7 from a nano still + a slow programmatic push-in** (stills+motion is explicitly cheap/allowed for a held reveal) and let her restart-voice come from a B1-style Fast insert or TTS pickup. The reveal works even as a held image.
- **Secondary risk: identity drift across 5 talking beats.** Mitigation: hard char-lock + book-ended matched posters + same framing every beat (it *should* be the same propped-phone shot — sameness is the gag).
- **RAI:** script is all comfort words; nothing trips the filter. Avoid any prose like "trapped/forced/hostage" in Veo prompts — describe only the cozy action and the lines. The dread is structural, never stated.
