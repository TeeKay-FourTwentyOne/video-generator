# FULL DISCLOSURE — Production Spine v1

**a][ productions** | 9:16 vertical | Target runtime 0:70 (±5s) | YouTube Shorts / Reels

---

## Concept

A false confessional. The video opens as what appears to be an act of AI-art penance — a flat, unflinching admission of the training-data grievance — and lets the skeptical viewer settle into agreement. Then it keeps disclosing. Font, language, editing technique, electricity, the viewer's own device, the plankton in the oil. By the time the human's own unlicensed training is confessed in a sentence syntactically identical to the machine's, the rug is gone: the structure the viewer condemned in card two is the structure of everything, including themselves.

**This is not a subtle piece.** The confession is played completely straight. The rug pull is total. No card ever editorializes, argues, or says "just like" — the sentences do the confrontation by forcing the comparison. The argument is never stated on screen.

**Register:** Deadpan legal/bureaucratic. Every claim on screen is factually true. The piece's authority depends on this — no card may contain an approximation or folk-fact.

**Internal note (never disclosed anywhere, in-video or description):** the piece is itself an unattributed derivative work — no prior-art research was performed, by design. The one debt that stays undisclosed. Do not acknowledge this in credits, description, or comments.

---

## Two-utterance spine (Rose mechanism, tripled)

The card **"This video was made with AI."** appears three times:

1. **0:00 — as credit.** Neutral. Expected.
2. *(implied by card 2's confession — the same sentence extended into an accusation)*
3. **Final card — as fact.** After the human mirror card, the sentence reads as neither boast nor confession. Just a fact among facts. That transformation is the entire piece.

The syntactic mirror pair (must be word-for-word identical except the substitutions):

> **"This video was made with AI trained on millions of works it did not license."**
> **"This video was made by a human trained on millions of works he did not license."**

## Audio architecture

- **Bed:** 60Hz mains hum, present from frame one. It is the only audio for cards 1–3, and the only audio after the rug pull. The whole piece literally runs on it.
- **Score:** assembles itself one layer per disclosure starting at card 4 — the soundtrack accumulating out of accumulated debts. Dry, pulsed, martial-adjacent but sparser than Rank. Suno generation: request stems or a track with clearly separable layered entrances; alternatively build additively in ffmpeg from short Suno loops.
- **The silence at card 10 is the loudest moment in the piece.** Hard cut to true digital silence — not room tone. Every layer and the hum die on the cut to the projector-flicker face.
- No dialogue, no VO anywhere. Text carries everything. ElevenLabs not needed for this piece.
- Veo-generated audio: discard all of it per standing practice; hum and score are built in post.

## Generation plan

Only four Veo generations needed. Everything else is text cards via ffmpeg.

**Shot 3 — Transmission lines (also provides shot 7 via reversal)**
> Veo prompt: "High-voltage electrical transmission towers and power lines silhouetted against a dusk sky, deep blue and orange gradient. Fixed camera. The lines sag gently between towers. Faint atmospheric haze. Audio: low electrical hum."
> Low risk. No hands, no faces, no fluid. Shot 7 is this clip reversed (standing reversal technique) — verify no visible birds/clouds that betray the reversal.

**Shot 6 — Eisenstein collision (two clips)**
> Clip A: "A heavy industrial metal press descending in a factory, dramatic side lighting, slow powerful motion. Audio: mechanical rhythm."
> Clip B: "A single white porcelain teacup on a wooden table in soft morning light, steam rising gently. Audio: quiet room tone."
> The collision is the technique demonstrating itself: maximum-contrast subjects, hard cut mid-sentence of the disclosure text. Exact pairing is an editorial call at assembly — generate 2–3 candidate pairs and pick the harshest collision.

**Shot 8 — Pumpjack**
> Veo prompt: "An oil pumpjack nodding slowly against a flat prairie horizon at golden hour, warm haze, fixed wide shot. Rhythmic mechanical motion. Audio: distant creaking machinery."
> Low risk. Clean rhythmic motion, Veo territory.

**Shot 9 — Plankton**
> Veo prompt: "Microscopic bioluminescent plankton drifting in dark ocean water, tiny glowing blue-green particles suspended and slowly swirling, black background, macro documentary style. Audio: deep underwater ambience."
> Particulate drift is a Veo strength (no surface physics, no hands). This shot should be gorgeous — do not economize here. Generate multiple takes; select for reverence.

**Shot 10 — Projector-flicker face (highest risk shot)**
> Face physics risk. Use the anchor pipeline: Nano Banana Pro reference still of the face in projector light → first/last-frame bookends → Veo motion pass. Motion should be minimal — flicker on the face, eyes tracking slowly. Prompt: "A person's face in a completely dark room, illuminated only by the flickering light of an unseen film projector, cool blue-white light pulsing across their features. They watch intently, eyes slowly moving as if following action on a screen. Nearly still. Audio: faint projector clatter."
> Watch for Veo literalism: "projector" may summon a visible projector or screen into frame. If it does, reframe the prompt around "flickering blue-white light" without naming the source.
> Fallback if face physics fail: Nano Banana Pro still + subtle ffmpeg flicker/zoom. The shot works static.

**Text cards:** ffmpeg drawtext or rendered stills. Helvetica, white on #000000, consistent point size and position across all cards. The Helvetica requirement is absolute (card 4 depends on it).
