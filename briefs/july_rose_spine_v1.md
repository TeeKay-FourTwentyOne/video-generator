## 1. Logline

A single mother finishes building a trellis on her house, is betrayed and evicted, and vanishes from the story. The new owners try to remove the trellis, fail, and years later stand admiring the climbing roses it carries — saying her name without knowing it.

## 2. Concept & Subtext (never on screen)

Quintilis was renamed Iulius months **after** Caesar's assassination. The name is not a monument he built; it is a residue he left. The people who defeated him could not get the word out of the calendar — not out of reverence, but because undoing the thing was more work than living with it.

The video says none of this. The Caesar connection lives entirely in the closing title card: **JULY**. Viewers who know, know. Everyone else gets a complete, quiet story about a woman named Rose.

**Register:** quiet, cold-warm, indifferent persistence. NOT inspirational ("they can never erase us" is forbidden territory). The echo wins by inertia, not devotion. Everyone on screen is innocent — the betrayer is weak, not villainous; the new owners are just buyers.

**The two-utterance spine.** Her name is spoken exactly twice, by two different people, in two different registers, and she is absent (or being erased) both times:

1. `"I'm sorry, Rose."` — the betrayer, at the moment of her destruction. Genuinely sorry. Weak. Human.
2. `"Oh, roses."` — a new owner, at the moment of bloom. Warm, casual, thoughtless. Zero weight.

The two readings should feel like they belong to different movies. The audience is the only one who hears them rhyme. **If the second voice sounds like it knows what it's saying, the video dies.**

## 3. Format

- 9:16 vertical, YouTube Shorts / Instagram Reels
- Target runtime: **60s** (acceptable window 45–75s)
- Dialogue: two lines only (see §7). All Veo audio muted and replaced.

## 4. Characters

- **ROSE** — 30s–40s, single mother. Capable hands. We read pride in her body language, not her face doing "acting." Wardrobe: practical, worn, one warm color note that will rhyme with the roses later (e.g., faded red-brown work shirt).
- **THE VOICE (Brutus)** — male, phone only, never seen. Flat, final, genuinely sorry.
- **NEW OWNERS** — a couple, 30s–50s, any configuration. Pleasant, unremarkable, innocent. One of them (OWNER A) performs both the tug-test and the final line.
- **(Optional, background only)** Rose's child — a small figure helping hand her tools in the build montage. Adds "single mother" without a single line. Cut if it complicates generation; the story survives without it.

## 6. Shot Notes & Veo Guidance

General:
- Clean physical descriptions; no poetry in prompts. Veo is literal.
- Watch trigger words: avoid "eviction," "foreclosure," "betrayal" in prompts (risk of on-screen text/graphic misfires and moderation friction). Describe the physical scene: "a printed notice taped to a front door."
- Do **not** include "Static camera angle unchanged from before" unless a shot specifically needs a locked camera (Beat 8 does — see below).
- Hands are load-bearing in Beats 1–2. Use closer, simpler compositions: one hand + one tool + one material per shot. Avoid complex two-hand fine-motor actions in a single prompt.
- Include audio notes in every Veo prompt as scratch reference only; all audio replaced in post.

Beat-specific:

**Beat 1 — Build montage (4–5 clips, ~3s each).** Suggested shots: (a) measuring and marking a cedar slat against house siding; (b) drill driving a screw, close on hand and bit; (c) fingers twisting wire around a junction; (d) wide low-angle, Rose on a step stool attaching the top rail, house facade behind. Golden but slightly overcast light — pride without sentimentality.

**Beat 2 — Finished.** Medium-wide from behind Rose, trellis on the house wall before her, bare (no plants yet — continuity gate: **the trellis must be plantless until Beat 8**). She wipes her hands on her jeans. Small exhale. No smile-to-camera.

**Beat 3 — The call.** She answers mid-glance at the trellis, half-smile. Frame: medium close, trellis soft in background. Her line collapses mid-sentence (see §7). Cut to nothing — we never see the caller.

**Beat 4 — Stillness.** The composition the first act exists to earn: Rose, phone lowered, small in frame against the full trellis. Hold 3–4s. This can be a Nano Banana Pro still with subtle ffmpeg push-in rather than a generated clip — saves credits and guarantees the framing.

**Beat 5 — Notice.** Close on a white printed notice taped to the front door, wind slightly lifting one corner. No readable text required beyond official-document texture; any legible name text is a generation liability — keep type small/soft. Rose does not appear.

**Beat 6 — New owners.** One clip: front walk, moving boxes, OWNER A and B carrying items toward the door. Trellis visible, bare, weathered slightly.

**Beat 7 — Tug-test.** OWNER A grips a trellis post and pulls twice. It doesn't move. A beat. Shrug, walks out of frame. Play it dry — no frustration performance. This is the whole thesis in one gesture: undoing her work is more effort than living with it.

**Beat 8 — Time-lapse.** Locked, fixed framing on the trellis (this shot DOES want the static-camera language). Progression: bare → green shoots threading the lattice → foliage → full red/pink bloom. Recommend building this as 4–6 Nano Banana Pro anchor stills on an identical plate, animated/blended via ffmpeg (crossfades + subtle light shifts), rather than asking Veo to hold a fixed frame across seasons. Composite-not-merge: keep the house plate constant, vary only the plant layer if possible.

**Beat 9 — Admiration.** Morning light. Owners with mugs, backs or three-quarter to camera, looking up at the bloomed trellis. OWNER A says the line. Warmth without ceremony. Do not cut to a meaningful close-up after the line — that would tell the audience it mattered. Hold the wide, let it be nothing.

**Beat 10 — Title card.** Hard cut to black. **JULY** in series title treatment. Hold 2s. No subtitle, no dedication, no rose motif in the type. The restraint is the point.

## 7. Audio Plan

**Dialogue (ElevenLabs, 2 lines + 1 fragment):**

- ROSE (Beat 3): `"...What? No. No, we trusted—"` — collapsing, cut off. Not a completed rhetorical question. The ground giving way, not a scene from a drama.
- THE VOICE (Beat 3): `"I'm sorry, Rose."` — flat, final, and genuinely sorry. Weak, human, not villainous. Brutus loved Caesar; that's the horror.
- OWNER A (Beat 9): `"Oh, roses."` — appreciative, casual, zero weight. Thoughtless warmth. Direction note for the read: the speaker is commenting on flowers, nothing more.

**Music (Suno):** single restrained cue, or two related cues split at the time jump. Sparse — piano/strings or ambient guitar, minor-adjacent but not mournful. **No swell at the final line or title card.** If anything, the music should thin out toward the end. Silence under "Oh, roses" is a legitimate option; score resumes/ends under the title card.

**SFX:** build sounds (drill, wire, wood), phone buzz, paper/wind for the notice, ambient birds/morning for Beats 8–9. All Veo audio muted.

## 8. Continuity & Anchor Plan (Nano Banana Pro)

- No plants on the trellis before Beat 8.
- Rose's warm-color wardrobe note should approximately match the bloom color — subtle, not matchy.
